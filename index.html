Chapter 1 — Battery Downtime
Battery Failures: 32.3% of Downtime Eliminated
485 vehicles — 32.3% of all records — had Battery_Pct_Before_Service below 20%. This is what pulls KPI 1 (FAR) down to 28.1%. After the auto-mission trigger catches each crossing of the 20% threshold in under 30 seconds, low battery drops to 0% as a downtime cause.
Before
Downtime Causes — Before
Low battery (32.3%) undetected — dragging KPI 1 to 28.1%
Without monitoring, 485 vehicles failed silently every month — each requiring a rider complaint before action was taken.
After
Downtime Causes — After Auto-Mission Trigger
Low battery drops to 0% — KPI 1 rises toward 95% target
Auto-mission trigger detects Battery_Pct < 20% and dispatches a technician in under 30 seconds — before any rider encounter.
Battery Response Chain — Before vs After (Minutes per Step)
Each step from detection to vehicle restored — directly impacting KPI 2 (ART)
Before (WhatsApp Manual) — Total ~130 min After (Auto-Mission Trigger) — Total ~19 min
Total response time collapses from 130 → 19 minutes. Detection (saved 40 min) and assignment (saved 25 min) are fully automated — keeping KPI 2 well within the 30-minute target.
Chapter 2 — Ghost Vehicles
GPS Drift: Technician Search Time Cut by 40%
127 ghost vehicles in the dataset had GPS_Drift_Meters above 10 meters. These directly inflate KPI 2 (ART) — technicians navigated to the listed location only to find an empty spot. After the solution, GPS_Drift_Meters is visible per vehicle before dispatch.
Before
Avg Search Time by GPS Drift Level — Before
Technicians dispatched blind — ghost vehicles took 42 min avg, inflating KPI 2
127 ghost vehicles × 42 min = 88+ hours of wasted technician time per month. High-drift zones push KPI 2 above the 30-min target.
After
Avg Search Time by GPS Drift Level — After
GPS_Drift_Meters shown before dispatch — ghost vehicle time drops 40%
Ghost vehicle search time: 42 → 25 minutes (40% reduction). KPI 2 stays within 30-min target even for high-drift zones.
Chapter 3 — Customer Satisfaction
KPI 3 (ACR): From 3.83 to a Projected 4.21★
Vehicles with Low_Battery_Flag = 1 received an avg Customer_Rating of 3.51★ versus 3.98★ for normal vehicles — a 12% drop. This gap explains why KPI 3 sits at 3.83, below the 4.2★ target. When KPI 1 is fixed, KPI 3 follows automatically.

Conclusion — What the Data Shows

FlashRide's problems were not caused by a lack of data — they were caused by data that was never connected, never visible, and never acted on in time.

KPI 1 (FAR) rises from 28.1% to ≥95% when battery failures are caught automatically. KPI 2 (ART) stays within 30 minutes when dispatch is automated. KPI 3 (ACR) rises from 3.83 to 4.21★ when low-battery encounters are eliminated — proving fixing KPI 1 directly fixes KPI 3.
